## 🛒 Ingredients
- ½ cup dried soybeans
- Cold water for soaking
- 1 litre boiling water

## 👩‍🍳 Directions
1. Soak beans 12 hours
2. Drain and Rinse
3. Put soybeans into blender
4. Add 1 litre boiling water
5. Blend
6. Pour into nut milk bag set over a bowl
7. Allow to cool before squeezing
8. Pour milk into boiler
9. Add large pinch of salt
10. Boil for 20 minutes, stirring frequently
11. I added in about 1 cup extra water slowly throughout the 20min of boiling in order to keep the volume of the finished milk from reducing as much.

## 📝 Notes
- ½ cup dried soybeans made roughly 200g soaked soybeans
- Added in 850g boiling water
- Made 700-750g strained soymilk, 3.5 cups
- After boiling, made total of 1 ¾ cups soymilk (8 cups = $2.20)
- Finished product tastes really good! Not beany! Was great in tea!
- Lipoxygenase could be inactivated and most of the beany flavor removed by either dropping unsoaked soybeans directly into boiling water or by removing any cracked or split beans prior to soaking, then carefully dropping the soaked beans into boiling water.
